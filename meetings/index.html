Summary

  • ben introduces himself: graduated from math major, transitioned to software industry and has been working for 2 years. intersted now in theoretical computer science.
  • caio introduces himself: fresh CS alumni; currently pursuing a master in compilers; motivated to study algorithms for code optimization.
  • lucper and caio are friends and had an internships together.
  • most members did not solve assignment 1 but they are planning for it the next week end.
  • for most members, this course is the first time they see proofs within the context of algorithms.
  • ben initiates a discussion, simple algorithm but tricky proof
    • mostafa addressed understanding a solution, and being able to creatively come up with a new, are totally two different skills.
    • lucper addresses that happens very commonly in combinatorics, A problem easily understood but very trikcy to solve it.
  • ben asks whether it is a pattern for greedy algorithms to be solved by induction
    • mostafa answers when an algorithm is formulated recursively or in terms of smaller instances of the same problem, Then induction is a very likely approach.
    • caio agrees what mostafa as he had a similar advice from his professor.
    • lucper pointes to algorithm design book by Kleinberg and Tardos for an alternative proof by contradiction
  • mostafa initaties a discussion, school systems are not the best environment for training on creatively coming up with novel ideas.
  • mostafa initaites a discussion, thinking of simpler problems or different versions as a problem solving strategy.
    • ben expresses this is his main strategy.
    • caio mentions a quote from how to prove it by George Polya.
    • lucper agrees.
  • mostafa ends up the with posing a question: If progressing slowly by thinking of related problems is our sole approach for progress, Is it possible to achieve breakthroughs or unexpected leaps?

Quotes

  • caio: if you cannot solve a problem, try to think of an easier version for it.
